Mission Statement

The mission of the MESA Program at College of the Canyons is to assist educationally underrepresented studentsin understanding and navigating the STEM transfer process in order to successfully graduate with a STEM degree, transfer to a four-year institution, and ultimately becomesignificant and positive contributors to the socioeconomic prosperity of their communities.

Amy - Program Director

Amy Foote is the program director, educational administrator, and mentor for MESA at College of the Canyons. Her goal is to provide a safe space for students to succeed professionally and academically for transfer to a four-year university. Amy went to Ventura Community College, where she found her interest in Marine Biology. Despite the lack of guidance through the college experience, she transferred to CSU Northridge and became a Biology lab technician for 13 years. She taught part-time at multiple college campuses before becoming a full-time director for MESA. Amy loved the meaning of MESA and recognized that experience was just as important as academic knowledge. The MESA skills lab now aims to provide hands-on experience for students in stem fields, giving them an edge when applying for internships and job interviewsâ€”constantly improving and renovating the learning facility with new equipment and safety precautions for the most optimal learning experience. Her new goal is to work with local universities to work with their department students that would like to mentor at MESA for a smooth transition to the four-year universities. As the Mesa program director, She has created a learning center to build your potential that you will take with you beyond College of the Canyons.
